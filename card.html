Giant Panda

Though it belongs to the order Carnivora, the giant panda's diet is over 99% bamboo.The giant panda lives in a few mountain ranges in central China, mainly in Sichuan, but also in neighbouring Shaanxi and Gansu.

  • Scientif Name: Ailuropoda melanoleuca, literally "black and white cat-foot".
  • Classification: True bear, part of the family Ursidae.
  • Average Length: Adults measure around 1.2 to 1.9 m (4 to 6 ft) long
  • Average Lifespan: Typically lives around 20 years in the wild and up to 30 years in captivity.

The giant panda has luxuriant black-and-white fur. The giant panda has a body shape typical of bears. It has black fur on its ears, eye patches, muzzle, legs, arms and shoulders. The rest of the animal's coat is white. Its paw has a "thumb" and five fingers; the "thumb" – actually a modified sesamoid bone – helps it to hold bamboo while eating.
